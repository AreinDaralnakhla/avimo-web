AViMO: Avatar in Motion

3D Human Reconstruction from Monocular Dance Videos with Expressive Motion

Arein Daralnakhla

Master's thesis

Imaging and Lighting Extended Reality (IMLEX)

Abstract

This project investigates the task of 3D human avatar reconstruction from monocular video, with a focus on dance performances featuring expressive and challenging body motion. Monocular video lacks depth and provides only a single viewpoint, making accurate 3D reconstruction difficult ‚Äî particularly under fast movement, self-occlusion, and motion blur common in performance art.

We begin with a comprehensive survey and evaluation of state-of-the-art methods across pose estimation, 3D reconstruction, and visualization tools. This analysis highlights key limitations in current pipelines: inaccurate pose estimation, reliance on slow multi-stage optimization, and difficulty producing accurate, reusable avatars suitable for animation and real-time use.

To address these challenges, we develop a unified, three-stage pipeline that integrates best-performing techniques across these tasks, with targeted improvements including enhanced hand pose estimation, accurate scene decomposition, and an efficient export for lightweight, animation-ready avatars.

Our evaluation demonstrates that the proposed pipeline balances speed, accuracy, and surface quality, making it well-suited for downstream applications such as motion retargeting, immersive media, and the preservation of cultural performances.

Our pipeline processes monocular dance videos through six main stages: video preprocessing with frame extraction, monocular depth estimation using MoGe, foreground-background segmentation with SAM v2.1, pose and shape estimation combining NLF and WiLoR for enhanced hand tracking, simultaneous training of human and scene 3D Gaussians, and finally animation export with interactive WebGL visualization using Spark renderer.
